SOUTHEASTERN SOCIOECONOMICS

For this project, I chose to focus on a number of categorical and quantitative demographic variables for counties in Virginia, North Carolina, South Carolina, Georgia, and Florida. Each data point represents a county (or in the case of Virginia, a county or county-equivalent city) with average literacy score, average numeracy score, leading employment industry, proportion of population without health insurance, unemployment rate, median household income, proportion of population with at least a four-year college degree, population total, violent crime rate, jail population rate, and proportion of the population below the national poverty line.
